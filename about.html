I am an undergraduate student, science educator, and early stage researcher with a strong interest in astronomy, astrophysics, and the physical understanding of the Universe. Over the past several years, I have actively engaged in astronomy education, research assistance, mentorship, and outreach, while continuously strengthening my theoretical and analytical foundation in physics and space science.

My academic interests are centered on observational cosmology, dark matter, and the large scale structure of the Universe. I am particularly interested in how galaxy groups and clusters can be used as tracers of underlying dark matter halos, and how environmental effects influence galaxy evolution.

Alongside formal education, I have pursued extensive independent learning, research oriented projects, and teaching experiences that reflect both my curiosity about the cosmos and my commitment to scientific growth.

Academic and Teaching Experience

I currently work as an Astronomy Tutor and Research Associate at WhyCosmos, where I teach astronomy and astrophysics through structured courses and personalized mentorship. My role involves simplifying complex scientific concepts, developing research based learning material, and mentoring students for national and international competitions and academic examinations.

Previously, I have worked as a Tutor on Schoolhouse.world, providing personalized instruction and academic guidance to students across multiple subjects. These experiences have strengthened my ability to communicate complex ideas clearly, track learning progress, and foster analytical thinking.

Research and Technical Experience

I have gained hands on exposure to applied research through my internship at Omspace Rocket Exploration Private Limited, where I supported aerospace and space technology projects. My work included assisting in the design and study of refractor telescope systems, liquid rocket engine concepts, and related simulations, applying principles of optics, propulsion, fluid dynamics, and electromagnetism.

Through these experiences, I developed skills in research documentation, technical analysis, and collaborative problem solving within multidisciplinary teams.

Projects

Leadership, Outreach, and Volunteering

I have held leadership roles in astronomy and science outreach initiatives, including serving as President of Astronomy Conservationist Wardha and AstroClub. Through these roles, I have organized stargazing sessions, educational events, competitions, and community awareness programmes focused on dark sky preservation and scientific literacy.

I have also participated in community service activities, including volunteering with ISKCON Temple initiatives focused on food and clothing distribution, strengthening my sense of social responsibility and teamwork.

Through a combination of education, research exposure, teaching, and outreach, I am building the skills and mindset required for research oriented environments. I aim to pursue advanced studies in physics or astronomy and contribute meaningfully to our understanding of the Universe.
